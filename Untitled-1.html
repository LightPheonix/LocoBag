Important elements of the game of Cricket

Cricket Pitch

It is 22 yd (20.12 m) long (1 chain) and 10 ft (3.05 m) wide. The surface is flat and is normally covered with extremely short grass, but can be completely dry or dusty soil with barely any grass or, in some circumstances (that are rarely seen in high level cricket), made from an artificial material.

Equipment

  • Ball – A red, white or pink ball with a cork base, wrapped in twine covered with leather.
  • Bat – A wooden bat is used.
  • Stumps – Three upright wooden poles that, together with the bails, form the wicket.
  • Bails – Two crosspieces made of wood, placed on top of the stumps.
  • Helmet - The helmet is designed to stop serious injury occuring due to the crickrt ball hitting the head of the batsman.
    • Batsman

    A player who is batting and are responsible for scoring the runs when it is their turn to bat. They must seize upon the bad deliveries from bowlers and protect their stumps at all times.

    Bowler

    In cricket, there are two broad categories of bowlers: pace and spin. Pace bowlers rely mostly on the speed of the ball to dismiss batsmen, whereas spin bowlers rely on the rotation of the ball. The bowler is a member of the fielding side who bowls or is bowling.

    Wicket Keeper

    A fielder in cricket who stands behind the wicket at which the ball is being bowled. The keeper can dismiss the batsman by using the ball to hit the stumps and remove the bails if the batsman is not in his crease after a delivery. The keeper then will knock out the bails and if the batsman is still outside the crease then he is out.
